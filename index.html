Museum Overview

The Smithsonian Natural History Museum in Washington, DC maintains one of the world's largest museum collections. The website lays out the research and collections of the Natural History Museum, featuring access to 148 million items and continuing to grow.

View Museum Collections

Context

The Natural History Museum serves as a vital research institution and cultural resource:

  • Smithsonian Natural History Museum DC: Located in Washington, DC, it houses extensive research facilities
  • Research & Collections: The website presents the research and collections of the museum, demonstrating its importance as an academic and public resource
  • Collection Scale: On the website, 148 million items are present from the museum collection which is still growing
  • Research Locations: The website also lays out the various locations used by the Smithsonian museums for research across multiple geographic areas

Target Users

Primary Audiences

  • Scientists: Anyone looking to the past for historical research and data
  • Students: Academic users seeking information and educational resources
  • Museum Patrons: Visitors looking to dive into the research part of the museum from another level besides visiting in person
  • Educators: School programs serve school students and educators to use and learn more about the collections

Website Content Analysis

Overview

The website describes the artifacts and collections in the Natural History Museum with the following key facts:

  • The size of the collection is 148 million items and still growing
  • Comprehensive organization of research materials and collections
  • Multiple access points for different user types

Strengths (Pros)

  • Navigation: The different sections are laid out on the side and in the middle of the webpage for the user to navigate easily
  • Information Architecture: Listing facts about the different parts of the museum for the user to pick up on at a glance
  • Depth of Content: Different tabs for the reader to dive deeper on specific topics
  • User Feedback: Tab for the user to give feedback on the website and suggest improvements
  • Event Listings: The museum lists daily events for the user to decide on what activities to attend

Areas for Improvement (Cons)

  • Pop-up Navigation: The side tab pops up every now and then pointing users to the different aspects of the website. It pops up on the different tabs of the website, which can be disruptive to the user experience
  • Recommended Fix: Consider reducing the frequency or making pop-ups optional to improve user experience while maintaining navigation accessibility

Conclusion

The Smithsonian Natural History Museum website successfully presents a vast collection of 148 million items with well-organized navigation and multiple access points for different user types. With minor improvements to pop-up frequency, the website would provide an even better user experience for researchers, students, educators, and museum visitors.
